About

Labradors, officially known as Labrador Retrievers, are one of the most popular and beloved dog breeds in the world. They are medium to large-sized dogs known for their friendly nature, intelligence, and versatility, making them excellent family pets, working dogs, and companions. Originating from Newfoundland, Canada, Labradors have a rich history as loyal helpers and are now celebrated for their adaptability to a wide variety of roles.

Physical Characters

Labradors are athletic and sturdy dogs, characterized by their balanced build and distinctive features:

Size: Labradors are medium to large-sized dogs. Male Labradors typically stand 22.5 to 24.5 inches tall and weigh between 65 to 80 pounds, while females are slightly smaller, standing 21.5 to 23.5 inches tall and weighing 55 to 70 pounds.

Coat: Labradors have a short, dense, and water-resistant double coat that helps them stay warm in colder conditions. Their coat is easy to maintain and sheds moderately.

The Labrador Retriever originated from the island of Newfoundland in Canada in the 1700s. They were initially bred to assist fishermen by retrieving fish and pulling in nets from the cold waters of the North Atlantic.
