About

Pugs are small, charming, and affectionate dogs known for their distinctive appearance and playful personality. With their wrinkled faces, round eyes, and curly tails, pugs are one of the most recognizable and adored dog breeds. They are often described as having a "big personality in a small body," making them an ideal companion for families, singles, and seniors alike.

Physical Characteristics

Pugs have a compact, sturdy, and muscular build that gives them a unique and endearing appearance:
Height: Pugs typically stand 10–13 inches (25–33 cm) tall at the shoulder.
Weight: They usually weigh 14–18 pounds (6–8 kg), although some may vary slightly.

Pugs have a long and storied history, dating back over 2,000 years to ancient China. They were originally bred as companion dogs for Chinese royalty and were highly valued for their flat faces and wrinkled features, which were thought to resemble good fortune. In the 16th century, pugs were brought to Europe by Dutch traders, where they quickly gained popularity among nobility and royal families. The breed became a symbol of wealth and status, and they were particularly adored by the House of Orange in the Netherlands and Queen Victoria of England. Today, pugs are cherished worldwide as lovable and loyal pets.
Pugs have moderate energy levels and need daily exercise, such as short walks or playtime. However, they are prone to overheating due to their short snouts, so care should be taken in hot or humid weather.
